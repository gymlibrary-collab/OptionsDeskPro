# Feature Spec — PRD-05: Language Audit of Strategy Narrative

**Date:** 15Jun2026
**Author:** Business Analyst
**Status:** Draft

---

## 1. Summary

OptionsDesk's plain-English strategy narrative is generated by `backend/services/interpreter.py` and rendered in the `StrategyNarrative` component on the Scanner tab's deep-analysis view. PRD-01 (Strategy Comparison Matrix, shipped 15 Jun 2026) removed the "AI Pick" ranking system from the scanner and analysis views. However, the narrative text itself was out of scope for PRD-01. Several phrases inside `interpreter.py` and `StrategyNarrative.tsx` still present OptionsDesk — or the system unattributed — as recommending or ranking a specific strategy for the user. Under Singapore's Financial Advisers Act and equivalent US investment adviser regulations these phrases constitute directive investment advice, which OptionsDesk is not licensed to provide.

This feature rewrites each offending phrase so the narrative remains educationally rich while making clear that:

1. OptionsDesk describes how strategies work in particular market conditions — it does not tell users what to trade.
2. Rules attributed explicitly to tastylive (a third-party trading educator) remain attributed to tastylive; their educational value is preserved.
3. No new fields, endpoints, or database changes are introduced — this is a pure text rewrite confined to `interpreter.py` and `StrategyNarrative.tsx`.

| Persona | Tier | Job-to-be-done |
|---------|------|----------------|
| Retail learner | free / starter | Read the strategy narrative to understand how a given strategy works under current market conditions, without being told what trade to make |
| Active paper trader | starter / pro | Use the narrative to evaluate whether a strategy's mechanics fit their own thesis, free from platform bias or directive language |
| Enterprise team lead | enterprise | Share narrative output with junior members without concern that the platform is giving unlicensed investment advice |
| Admin (platform owner) | admin / enterprise | Confirm that all user-facing text meets the regulatory requirement established by PRD-01 before any further user acquisition |

## 3. Functional Requirements

1. The phrase `ranks as the best-fit strategy` (line 386 of `interpreter.py`, inside the `else` fallback of `_why_this_strategy()`) must be removed. The replacement must describe why the strategy aligns with the current IV environment and directional bias without asserting a ranking or recommendation.

2. The phrase `this strategy remains the recommendation given current IV and bias conditions` (line 1197 of `interpreter.py`, in the `trade_plain_english` field of the error-path return in `generate_narrative()`) must be removed. The replacement must convey that the strategic analysis is still valid without using the word "recommendation."

3. The unattributed phrase `The recommended exit point is` (line 648 of `interpreter.py`, in `_profit_scenario()`) must be rewritten to attribute the exit rule to tastylive or to present it as an educational convention rather than a platform directive.

4. The phrase `tastylive recommends keeping this position to 1–3% of your total portfolio and using the 2× credit rule as a stop` (line 309, in `_why_this_strategy()`) must retain its tastylive attribution. No change is required to the content or attribution of this phrase.

5. The phrases `tastylive recommends closing early` (line 580), `tastylive recommends NOT waiting for maximum profit` (line 638), and `tastylive recommends closing when you've captured 50%` (line 644) must retain their tastylive attribution. No change is required to the content or attribution of these phrases.

6. The phrase `tastytrade (best for beginners)` (line 921, in `_execution_checklist()`) must have the parenthetical `(best for beginners)` removed. Factual platform names may remain; unattributed quality rankings of third-party products may not.

7. The phrase `At {dte} DTE this expiry sits in that optimal window` (line 941, in `_execution_checklist()`) must replace the word "optimal" with neutral descriptive language. A factual description of why 30–45 DTE is a commonly used window in tastylive's framework may be preserved and attributed to tastylive.

8. The two fallback strings in `StrategyNarrative.tsx` (lines 229 and 257) that read `The strategy recommendation above is still valid.` must be rewritten to remove the word "recommendation." The replacement must convey that the strategic analysis applies even without live chain data.

9. All other language in `interpreter.py` that is attributed to tastylive (position-sizing rules, the 2× credit rule, the 21-DTE close rule, the 50% profit-target rule, rolling for credit) must be preserved as-is, including the tastylive attribution. Removing or paraphrasing tastylive-attributed educational rules is out of scope and would reduce the educational value of the platform.

10. The rewritten phrases must not introduce any new directive, ranking, or advisory framing. After the audit, no unattributed phrase in the narrative output may assert that a strategy is "recommended," "the best," "optimal," "ideal," or "the top pick" for the user's situation.

11. No changes to API response shapes, database schema, or subscription tier logic are permitted under this PRD. The `why_this_strategy` field in the narrative JSON response keeps the same key name; only its string content changes.

## 4. User Stories & Acceptance Criteria

### Story 1 — Fallback strategy description uses no ranking language

**As a** retail learner reading the "Why This Strategy" section, **I want** the explanation of how this strategy fits current conditions to describe the fit without claiming it is the best or highest-ranked strategy **so that** I make my own decision about whether to use it.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- [ ] AC-1.1: Open a ticker that routes to the `else` fallback branch of `_why_this_strategy()` (a strategy key not explicitly listed in the `if/elif` chain — e.g. `calendar_spread` or any strategy key absent from the explicit blocks). Confirm the rendered "Why This Strategy" panel does not contain the phrases "ranks as," "best-fit strategy," "recommended strategy," "top strategy," "optimal strategy," or "ideal strategy."
- [ ] AC-1.2: The rewritten fallback text still contains the IV rank value and the directional bias label from the analysis, confirming the educational content is preserved.
- [ ] AC-1.3: The rewritten text remains grammatically complete and reads as a coherent explanation of how the strategy aligns with the stated IV environment and directional bias.

### Story 2 — Error-path narrative uses no recommendation language

**As a** paper trader who receives a narrative when live options chain data is unavailable, **I want** the trade description to acknowledge the data gap without claiming the platform is still recommending the strategy **so that** I am not misled about the nature of the output.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- [ ] AC-2.1: Simulate or trigger the error path in `generate_narrative()` (e.g. by passing a trade dict with an `error` key set). Confirm the `trade_plain_english` field in the response does not contain the word "recommendation" or any synonym phrasing ("still the pick," "still advised," "still suggested").
- [ ] AC-2.2: The rewritten error-path text still conveys that the IV and bias analysis in the other narrative sections (market snapshot, IV context, why this strategy) applies to the symbol and is not invalidated by the missing chain data.
- [ ] AC-2.3: The text is under 200 words and completes in a single paragraph.

### Story 3 — Exit-point language is attributed or neutralised

**As a** paper trader reading the "Profit Scenario" section, **I want** the exit-point guidance to be presented as an educational convention from tastylive rather than a platform directive **so that** I understand the source of the rule and retain the freedom to apply my own exit plan.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- [ ] AC-3.1: Open any ticker with a live options chain. Navigate to the "If It Works — Profit Scenario" panel. Confirm the text does not contain the phrase "The recommended exit point is."
- [ ] AC-3.2: The rewritten text still communicates the specific profit-target percentage and dollar value for the strategy (e.g. "50% of max profit = $X").
- [ ] AC-3.3: If the rewritten text attributes the exit rule to tastylive, the attribution is explicit (e.g. "tastylive's exit rule," "under the tastylive framework," or equivalent). If the rewrite omits attribution, it presents the exit target as a factual trade parameter computed from the strategy's profit-target percentage, not as advice.

### Story 4 — Platform name endorsement removed from execution checklist

**As a** retail learner reading the step-by-step execution guide, **I want** the list of broker platforms to be factual and neutral **so that** the platform is not making unattributed quality judgements about third-party products on my behalf.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- [ ] AC-4.1: Open any ticker with a live options chain that produces a populated execution checklist. Locate step 1 (the broker-opening step). Confirm the text does not contain the phrase "best for beginners" or any parenthetical quality assessment of a named broker.
- [ ] AC-4.2: The four broker platform names (tastytrade, thinkorswim by Schwab, Interactive Brokers, E*TRADE Power E*TRADE) remain in the text — only the quality assessment parenthetical is removed.
- [ ] AC-4.3: The step reads grammatically correctly after the parenthetical is removed.

### Story 5 — DTE window described factually or attributed, not labelled "optimal"

**As a** retail learner reading the step-by-step execution guide, **I want** the explanation of why the 30–45 DTE window is used to be presented as a factual or tastylive-attributed convention **so that** the platform is not asserting an unattributed quality judgement about trade timing.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- [ ] AC-5.1: Open any ticker with a live options chain. Locate the expiration-date selection step in the execution checklist. Confirm the text does not contain the word "optimal" in reference to the DTE window.
- [ ] AC-5.2: The rewritten text still explains the 30–45 DTE range and the reason time decay behaves differently in that window. The factual explanation (theta acceleration, gamma risk profile) is preserved.
- [ ] AC-5.3: If the rewrite attributes the 30–45 DTE convention to tastylive, the attribution is explicit and consistent with the attribution style used elsewhere in the narrative.

### Story 6 — Frontend fallback panels use no recommendation language

**As a** paper trader who sees the profit or loss scenario panel in a "data unavailable" state, **I want** the fallback message to describe what is missing without calling the strategy a "recommendation" **so that** the frontend is consistent with the backend language audit.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- [ ] AC-6.1: Simulate a narrative response where `profit_scenario` is an empty string (trigger the error-path return from `generate_narrative()` or pass a strategy whose chain build fails). Confirm the fallback text in the profit scenario panel does not contain the word "recommendation."
- [ ] AC-6.2: Simulate a narrative response where `loss_scenario` is an empty string. Confirm the fallback text in the loss scenario panel does not contain the word "recommendation."
- [ ] AC-6.3: Both fallback texts still communicate (a) that the scenario requires live options chain data and (b) that the analysis sections above the panel remain valid.

### Story 7 — Tastylive-attributed language is fully preserved

**As a** retail learner relying on tastylive-sourced educational rules for managing positions, **I want** all tastylive-attributed guidance (position sizing, 2× credit rule, 21-DTE close, 50% profit target, rolling for credit) to remain unchanged **so that** the educational content of the platform is not degraded by the audit.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- [ ] AC-7.1: Open any ticker that produces an undefined-risk strategy narrative (e.g. `short_naked_put`, `short_strangle`). Confirm the "Why This Strategy" section still contains tastylive's 1–3% portfolio sizing rule and the 2× credit rule language.
- [ ] AC-7.2: Open any ticker with a credit-strategy narrative. Confirm the profit scenario section still contains language referencing the 50% of max profit exit convention attributed to tastylive.
- [ ] AC-7.3: Open any ticker with a credit-strategy execution checklist. Confirm the 21-DTE close rule step is present and unchanged.
- [ ] AC-7.4: Open any ticker. In the "The Trade in Simple Terms" panel, confirm the early-exit reference (line 580 region) still attributes the guidance to tastylive.

## 6. Out of Scope

- Any change to the strategy catalog in `strategy_engine.py` (strategy names, fit scores, DTE targets, pop ranges).
- Any change to the Strategy Comparison Matrix introduced by PRD-01.
- Any change to API response field names or shapes (the `why_this_strategy` key remains; only its string value changes).
- Any change to the database schema, subscription tiers, or tier limit logic.
- Any change to the `_ai_narrative()` function or any Claude API call (none exist in `interpreter.py` — the narrative engine is entirely rule-based Python; this is confirmed by the file's docstring: "No AI, no approximations").
- Audit of the `market_context.py` service or any other backend service outside `interpreter.py`.
- Audit of any component other than `StrategyNarrative.tsx` in the frontend.
- Audit of narrative text delivered via the `UserGuide.tsx` component.
- Changes to the `TradingDesk.tsx` Reddit buzz feed or any other tab's copy.
- Systematic legal review of the full platform — this audit is scoped to the six specific phrases identified in section 5.
- Adding a disclaimer banner to the narrative view — that is a separate product decision.
- Changing tastylive-attributed language, including phrasings that use the word "recommends" when the subject of the sentence is "tastylive" and the attribution is explicit and unambiguous.

## 7. Edge Cases & Failure Modes

| Scenario | Expected Behaviour |
|----------|-------------------|
| Strategy key falls into the `else` fallback of `_why_this_strategy()` | The rewritten fallback phrase (requirement 1) is rendered. No ranking language appears. |
| `trade['error']` is set (chain data unavailable) | The rewritten error-path `trade_plain_english` string (requirement 2) is returned. No "recommendation" language appears. |
| `profit_scenario` or `loss_scenario` is an empty string (frontend render) | The rewritten frontend fallback text (requirement 8) is displayed. No "recommendation" language appears. |
| Strategy key is explicitly handled in `_why_this_strategy()` (not the `else` branch) | None of the explicitly handled branches (lines 320–381) contain the phrases targeted by this audit. No change required. These branches are not in scope. |
| Execution checklist is empty (chain fetch fails, `execution_checklist` returns `[]`) | The execution checklist section is hidden by the existing frontend guard (`narrative.execution_checklist.length === 0`). The broker platform language (requirement 6) and DTE language (requirement 7) are only rendered when the checklist is populated. |
| Market data unavailable (all three tiers fail, synthetic chain used) | Synthetic chain still produces a populated execution checklist (`_synthetic=True` flag is set but checklist still generates). Requirements 6 and 7 apply and must pass on synthetic-chain outputs. |
| Admin user | Admin sees the identical narrative as any authenticated user. No admin-specific narrative path exists. |

## 10. Open Questions

**OQ-1 — Attribution style for the exit-point rewrite (requirement 3 / item C).**
Two valid approaches exist for line 648: (a) attribute the exit rule explicitly to tastylive ("tastylive's exit target for this strategy is…"), or (b) remove the directive framing entirely and present it as a computed trade parameter ("This trade's profit target is…"). Both satisfy the regulatory requirement. The architect should select one approach and apply it consistently across all unattributed exit-point language. This is a design decision, not a requirements decision, and is deferred to Gate 2.

**OQ-2 — Consistency check for narrative text not reachable via automated test.**
Some narrative branches (e.g. `call_broken_wing_butterfly`, `reverse_big_lizard`) require specific IV/bias combinations that may not be reproducible with the test suite's mock data. The QA engineer (Gate 4) should confirm that the full set of `if/elif` branches in `_why_this_strategy()` are covered by at least one test case, or document which branches cannot be reached and confirm they do not contain any of the phrases in scope.

**OQ-3 — Scope of "recommendation" in frontend copy beyond `StrategyNarrative.tsx`.**
The audit identified two fallback strings in `StrategyNarrative.tsx`. A broader search of all `.tsx` components has not been performed under this PRD. If the security reviewer (Gate 5) identifies further instances in `StrategyDetail.tsx`, `TradePanel.tsx`, or elsewhere, those should be logged as a follow-on task rather than expanded into this PRD's scope.
