Why we use delayed data — and why it barely matters

Options Compass uses Yahoo Finance data, which carries an approximate 15-minute delay for options quotes. This keeps subscription costs low — no expensive real-time exchange feeds.

For learning options strategy, a 15-minute delay is near real-time enough. You can see exactly how a strategy plays out against live market conditions, track your paper P&L as prices move, and build genuine conviction — all at a fraction of the cost of a live data feed.

What you learn here transfers directly to real trading

The platform calculates which options strategies fit the current market conditions — IV environment, directional bias, earnings risk — and ranks them for you. Paper trade the recommendations, watch how each position evolves against real price moves, and close trades with a clear read on what worked and why. That cycle of seeing, doing, and reviewing is what builds real confidence to trade with actual capital.

  • Strategy selection logic and strike choice are identical with delayed or live data
  • Greeks (delta, theta, vega, gamma) are model-calculated — unaffected by data delay
  • Position sizing, stop rules, and roll mechanics are the same at any latency
  • Earnings awareness and expiry selection are calendar-driven, not tick-driven
  • Paper P&L tracks against real prices — you see exactly how the trade would have performed
How It Works

From zero to your first options trade in minutes

No prior options knowledge required. The platform guides you at every step.

1

Pick any ticker

Type any stock symbol. Options Compass pulls the full chain, quote, and market context automatically.

2

Read the AI recommendation

The engine analyses IV environment, directional bias, and earnings risk — then recommends the best-fit strategy from 31 professional setups.

3

Understand the trade

A plain-English narrative explains what the trade is, why it fits, what needs to go wrong for it to lose, and how to manage it.

4

Paper trade it

Enter the trade with one click. It lands in your paper portfolio at real market prices. Track P&L, Greeks, and risk signals as the market moves.

5

Review and improve

The AI trade journal reviews your closed trades against established methodology rules — spotting patterns in what you did right and wrong.
